Preparation Guide — Last 3 Months + Exam Day

2027 Strategy
Physics
80+/100
Mechanics + Electrodynamics carry ~55% weightage. Master these first.
Chemistry
90+/100
Highest scoring subject — Inorganic + Physical = quick wins. Don't ignore NCERT.
Mathematics
75+/100
Calculus + Coordinate Geometry = 40% of paper. Practice 50+ problems daily.
Combined Goal: 245+ marks for safe 99+ percentile across any shift difficulty.

Last 3 Months Strategy

1
Revision Cycle: Complete 3 full revisions — short, medium, and deep. Don't start new topics.
2
Mock Tests: Take 2–3 full-length papers every week with detailed post-mortem. Simulate real exam pressure.
3
Weak Areas: Block fixed days for your 2–3 weakest chapters. Targeted drilling outperforms general revision.
4
Previous Papers: Solve at least 5 years of JEE Main papers (2022–2026). Pattern recognition is a real edge.
5
Health: 7–8 hours of sleep is non-negotiable in the final 30 days. Cognitive performance drops hard without it.

Exam-Day Game Plan

→
Section Order: Pick your strongest subject first — early confidence boosts performance throughout.
→
Time Allocation: ~50–55 minutes per subject; 15–20 min buffer at end. Don't get stuck on one question.
→
Question Selection: Attempt sure-shot questions first; mark doubtful ones for review. Skip and return.
→
Risk Management: Negative marking applies to MCQs AND NVTs — avoid pure guessing. −1 adds up fast.
→
Last 15 Minutes: Switch to review-only mode. Resist attempting new questions in panic. Guard your score.
→
Mindset: Even toppers don't attempt all 75. Accuracy beats volume every time.
Success Mantra: Consistent practice + smart shift-aware strategy + composed exam-day execution = high JEE Main percentile. NTA's normalisation gives every candidate a fair playing field. Your job: maintain maximum accuracy. The system handles the rest.
